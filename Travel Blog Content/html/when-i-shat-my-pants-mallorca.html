When I Shat My Pants in Mallorca

AM
Alex M.
Mar 6, 2026 · 8 min

She made me drink Kefir. She couldn't have known it was going to end like this. I did.

Bad things were brewing in my stomach. I couldn't hold it in much longer.

My hands trembled as I gripped the wheel ever harder. We were so close to the supermarket, I could practically see the bathroom sign glowing like a beacon of hope. But then, in a moment of pure genius, I decided to tell Christine a joke. You know, to lighten the mood. Big mistake. The laughter (hers, not mine) cost us precious seconds. Seconds I did not have.

As we screeched into the parking lot, I did what any rational adult would do: I bailed. I left Christine in the car, clutching the keys and her dignity, while I sprinted for the entrance like a man possessed. I burst through the doors, locked eyes with the cashier, and blurted out, "Where's the bathroom?" She pointed upstairs. I ran. Oh, how I ran.

But fate, and kefir, had other plans. Halfway up the stairs, disaster struck. It was too late. There, in the middle of a Spanish supermarket, I made a deposit no one wanted. I stood there, defeated, contemplating my life choices and the perils of fermented dairy.

Eventually, I cleaned myself up as best I could. Christine, ever the hero, came to my rescue with a fresh pair of pants and only a little bit of judgment in her eyes. That, my friends, is the last time I drink kefir before a road trip. Mallorca: beautiful beaches, questionable life decisions.
